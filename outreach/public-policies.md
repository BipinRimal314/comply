## NCUA Credit Union Directory and Data

### Credit Union Locator (Search Tool)
- **URL:** https://mapping.ncua.gov/ResearchCreditUnion
- **What it does:** Search credit unions by name, address, zip code, or charter number. View branch info, phone, web address.
- **Use for outreach:** Look up specific credit unions and find their website to check for published policies.

### NCUA Open Data Portal
- **URL:** https://ncua.gov/data
- **What it does:** Central hub for NCUA data resources. Links to analysis tools, locator, and call report data.

### Call Report Quarterly Data (Bulk Download)
- **URL:** https://ncua.gov/analysis/credit-union-corporate-call-report-data/quarterly-data
- **What it does:** Download ZIP files containing CSV data for all federally insured credit unions. Includes membership, assets, loans, and other financial metrics.
- **Format:** Comma-delimited text files (CSV) in ZIP archives. Available from March 1994 onward.
- **Use for outreach:** Get a complete list of all ~4,700 active federally insured credit unions with basic financial data. Filter by asset size to target credit unions most likely to need compliance tooling.

### Historical Credit Union Data
- **URL:** https://ncua.gov/analysis/credit-union-corporate-call-report-data/ncua-quarterly-us-map-review/historical-credit-union-data-us
- **What it does:** Historical data from Q2 2016 through Q3 2025 in CSV and XLSX formats.

### CUOnline Data Web Service (API)
- **URL:** https://ncua.gov/regulation-supervision/regulatory-reporting/cuonline
- **What it does:** Bulk data API, same data as quarterly downloads but dynamic/real-time. Free. Typically used by data aggregators.
- **Contact:** BImail@ncua.gov for access questions.

### Data.gov NCUA Datasets
- **URL:** https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/ncua
- **What it does:** 25+ NCUA datasets available through Data.gov, including 5300 Call Report data.

### California DFPI — State-Chartered Credit Union Directory
- **URL:** https://dfpi.ca.gov/regulated-industries/credit-unions/directory-of-state-chartered-credit-unions/
- **What it does:** Lists all California state-chartered credit unions with license numbers, officer names, addresses, phone numbers, and assigned DFPI contacts.
- **Use for outreach:** State-level directories include officer names and direct contacts.

## Finding Compliance Officer Contacts

There is no single public directory of credit union compliance officers. Here are the best approaches:

### Free Methods
1. **NCUA Call Report Data** — Download the quarterly CSV. Filter to credit unions in your target asset range. Visit each credit union's website to find the compliance officer (often listed on the "About" or "Leadership" page, or in published BSA policies like the Fayette County School Employees CU example above).
2. **State Regulator Directories** — California DFPI, Texas CUD, and other state regulators publish directories with officer names.
3. **LinkedIn Search** — Search "BSA Compliance Officer" + "credit union" to find individuals. Free LinkedIn limits outreach volume.
4. **Credit Union League Membership Lists** — Contact state/regional leagues (e.g., Carolinas Credit Union League, Cooperative Credit Union Association) for member directories.
5. **Published Policies** — Some credit unions name their BSA officer in published policy documents (as Fayette County School Employees CU does).

### Paid Methods
1. **ZoomInfo** — Has credit union compliance officer contact data. Expensive ($15K+/year).
2. **RocketReach** — Individual contact lookup. Pay-per-search model.
3. **AverickMedia** — Sells curated credit union email lists by job title (BSA officers, compliance managers, directors). BPA-audited.
4. **Credit Union National Association (CUNA/America's Credit Unions)** — May sell or provide member directories to vendors.

### Professional Networks
- **America's Credit Unions (formerly CUNA)** — https://www.americascreditunions.org/ — Industry association, conferences, certification programs (CUCO designation).
- **NAFCU** — https://www.nafcu.org/ — BSA Conference and compliance resources.
- **Cooperative Credit Union Association (CCUA)** — https://www.ccua.org/compliance — Regional compliance support.
- **Carolinas Credit Union League** — https://carolinasleague.org/risk/risk-management/bsa-aml-resources/ — BSA/AML resources for member credit unions.

## Testing Strategy

### Priority Documents for Compliance Linting Tests

The best test documents are the ones that contain the most compliance-relevant content:

1. **Fayette County School Employees CU — BSA Policy** (Category 1) — Pure BSA/AML policy, most directly relevant
2. **Altamaha FCU — BSA/AML/OFAC Policy** (Category 1) — Second BSA-specific policy
3. **Beacon CU / Union Fidelity FCU — Full Disclosure Booklets** (Category 2) — Multi-regulation coverage
4. **Alliant CU — Account Agreement** (Category 2) — Large, recent, comprehensive
5. **CUES Sample Policies** (Category 6) — Clean templates, good for baseline testing

### What to Lint For

Based on the regulatory documents in Category 7, key compliance gaps to detect:

- **BSA/AML:** Missing CDD/EDD procedures, missing beneficial ownership requirements, outdated SAR filing timelines, incomplete CIP procedures, missing OFAC screening language, absent independent testing requirements
- **Reg E (EFT):** Missing error resolution procedures, incomplete liability limitations, missing initial disclosure requirements
- **Reg CC (Funds Availability):** Missing hold notification procedures, incomplete exception hold language
- **Reg DD (Truth in Savings):** Missing APY calculations, incomplete fee disclosures
- **GLBA Privacy:** Missing opt-out rights, incomplete information sharing categories
- **Vendor Management:** Missing due diligence requirements, absent ongoing monitoring, incomplete contract provisions
- **Record Retention:** Missing minimum retention periods, absent destruction procedures

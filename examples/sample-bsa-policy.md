# Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering Policy

**Last Reviewed:** 2026-01-10
**Next Review Date:** 2027-01-15
**Approved By:** Board of Directors
**Version:** 3.2

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the Bank Secrecy Act and Anti-Money Laundering compliance program for [Institution Name] in accordance with 31 USC 5311 et seq., the USA PATRIOT Act (Public Law 107-56), and implementing regulations at 31 CFR Chapter X.

## 2. BSA Compliance Program — Five Pillars

### 2.1 Internal Controls

The institution shall maintain a system of internal controls to ensure ongoing compliance with BSA/AML requirements. Internal controls include:

- Policies, procedures, and processes to identify and report suspicious activity
- Dual control and segregation of duties for BSA-related functions
- Monitoring systems for unusual transaction patterns
- Quality assurance reviews of filed reports

### 2.2 Independent Testing

The BSA/AML compliance program shall undergo independent testing at least annually. Testing shall be conducted by qualified internal audit staff or an external party with no involvement in BSA/AML operations.

The scope of independent testing shall include:
- Evaluation of the overall adequacy of the compliance program
- Transaction testing to verify suspicious activity monitoring
- Review of SAR and CTR filing processes
- Assessment of training effectiveness

### 2.3 Designated BSA Compliance Officer

The Board of Directors shall appoint a designated BSA compliance officer with sufficient authority and resources to implement and maintain the compliance program. The BSA Officer reports directly to the Board and shall:

- Oversee day-to-day BSA/AML compliance operations
- Ensure timely filing of all required reports with FinCEN
- Coordinate with law enforcement and regulatory agencies
- Maintain current knowledge of BSA/AML regulatory developments

### 2.4 Training

All employees shall receive BSA/AML training appropriate to their job functions:

- **New employee training:** Within 30 days of hire
- **Annual training:** All staff, with completion documented
- **Board of Directors:** Annual briefing on BSA/AML program status
- **Role-based training:** Specialized training for tellers, loan officers, and compliance staff
- **Training records:** Maintained for a minimum of five years

### 2.5 Customer Due Diligence

#### Customer Identification Program (CIP)

Per 31 CFR 1020.220, the institution shall verify the identity of each customer opening an account. Identification requirements include:

- Name, date of birth, address, and identification number
- Verification procedures using documentary and non-documentary methods
- Recordkeeping of information obtained for five years after account closure
- Screening against government lists (OFAC SDN list, 314(a) requests)
- Customer notice that identification is being requested to comply with federal law

#### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Per 31 CFR 1010.230, the institution shall:

- Identify and verify the identity of beneficial ownership of legal entity customers (25% ownership threshold)
- Understand the nature and purpose of customer relationships to develop a risk rating
- Conduct ongoing monitoring of customer activity for suspicious transactions
- Apply enhanced due diligence to high-risk customers, including Politically Exposed Persons
- Document the basis for risk rating determinations

## 3. Suspicious Activity Reporting

### 3.1 SAR Filing Requirements

Per 31 CFR 1020.320, the institution shall file a Suspicious Activity Report with FinCEN when:

- A transaction involves $5,000 or more and the institution knows or suspects fraud, money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illegal activity
- The filing deadline is 30 calendar days from initial detection (60 days if no suspect is identified)
- Supporting documentation shall be maintained for five years from the date of filing
- SAR confidentiality shall be maintained; no disclosure to the subject of the report

### 3.2 SAR Narrative Requirements

Each SAR narrative shall include:
- Who conducted the suspicious activity
- What instruments or mechanisms were used
- When the activity occurred
- Where the activity took place
- Why the activity is suspicious
- How the activity was conducted

## 4. Currency Transaction Reporting

Per 31 CFR 1010.310, the institution shall file a Currency Transaction Report for each transaction in currency exceeding $10,000. CTR requirements include:

- Aggregation of multiple currency transactions by or on behalf of a single person during a business day
- Filing within 15 calendar days of the transaction
- Exemptions for certain eligible customers per 31 CFR 1020.315
- Monitoring for structured transactions designed to evade CTR filing thresholds

## 5. OFAC Compliance

The institution shall maintain an OFAC compliance program that includes:

- Screening of all customers, transactions, and counterparties against the OFAC SDN list and other sanctions lists
- Blocking and rejecting transactions involving blocked persons or prohibited countries
- Procedures for handling potential OFAC matches (true positives and false positives)
- Record retention of all OFAC screening results
- Reporting of blocked or rejected transactions to OFAC within 10 business days

## 6. Recordkeeping

All records related to BSA/AML compliance shall be retained for a minimum of five years, including:

- SARs and supporting documentation (five-year retention from filing date)
- CTRs (five-year retention from filing date)
- CIP records (five years after account closure)
- CDD and beneficial ownership records (five years after account closure)
- Training records (five years)
- Independent testing reports (five years)

## 7. Board Reporting

The BSA Officer shall provide the Board of Directors with:

- Quarterly reports on BSA/AML program activity
- Annual risk assessment updates
- Summary of examination findings and corrective actions
- Notification of significant regulatory changes
